GIRAFFES

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, characterized by their long necks and spotted coat patterns. They belong to the genus Giraffa in the family Giraffidae. There is only one species of giraffe, Giraffa camelopardalis, but several subspecies exist.

Giraffes are native to various countries in Africa, including savannahs, woodlands, and grasslands. They are herbivores, feeding primarily on leaves and buds from tall trees, especially acacia trees.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

You will find the giraffes in the African Savannah exhibit, near the baobab tree.
