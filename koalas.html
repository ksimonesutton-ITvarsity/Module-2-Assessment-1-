KOALAS

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia. They are members of the family Phascolarctidae and are known for their distinctive appearance, with a stout body, large ears, and a broad, spoon-shaped nose.

Koalas primarily inhabit eucalypt forests and woodlands, where they feed almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves. They are well adapted to a arboreal lifestyle, spending most of their time in trees.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

You will find the koalas in the Australian Outback exhibit, nestled among the eucalyptus trees.
