GEMSBOK

Gemsboks are large antelopes native to Southern Africa. They are part of the genus Oryx in the family Bovidae. Their distinctive features include long, straight horns and striking black and white facial markings.

Gemsboks are well-adapted to arid environments and are commonly found in the Kalahari Desert and other semi-desert regions. They are herbivores, primarily feeding on grasses, leaves, and roots.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

You will find the gemsboks in the African Plains exhibit, near the rocky outcrop.
